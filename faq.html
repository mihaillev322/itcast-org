Frequently asked questions about the IT cast

Who plays Pennywise in the IT cast?

Bill Skarsgård portrays Pennywise the Dancing Clown in both IT Chapter One (2017) and IT Chapter Two (2019), taking over the iconic role originally played by Tim Curry in the 1990 miniseries. Skarsgård's performance is characterised by physical contortion, vocal modulation, and an unsettling presence that distinguishes his interpretation from earlier versions. His casting was announced in 2016, and he worked closely with director Andy Muschietti to develop the character's movements and mannerisms.

What child actors make up the Losers Club in the IT cast?

The young Losers Club features Jaeden Martell (Bill Denbrough), Finn Wolfhard (Richie Tozier), Sophia Lillis (Beverly Marsh), Jack Dylan Grazer (Eddie Kaspbrak), Chosen Jacobs (Mike Hanlon), Jeremy Ray Taylor (Ben Hanscom), and Wyatt Oleff (Stan Uris) as the group of kids who confront Pennywise in Derry. These seven actors formed the emotional core of IT Chapter One and returned for flashback sequences in IT Chapter Two, ensuring continuity across both films.

How does the IT Chapter Two cast differ from Chapter One?

IT Chapter Two features adult versions of the Losers Club played by Jessica Chastain (Beverly), James McAvoy (Bill), Bill Hader (Richie), Isaiah Mustafa (Mike), Jay Ryan (Ben), James Ransone (Eddie), and Andy Bean (Stan), while the child actors return in flashback scenes. The adult cast was carefully selected to match the physical and emotional qualities of their younger counterparts, creating a seamless transition between timelines. Director Andy Muschietti has stated that casting the adult ensemble was one of the most challenging aspects of pre-production, as each actor needed to honour the work of the child performers while bringing their own depth to the roles.

What Stephen King adaptation features the most well-known ensemble cast?

The IT films are widely regarded as having one of the most impressive ensemble casts among Stephen King adaptations, blending established stars like Jessica Chastain and Bill Hader with breakout young talent. The dual-timeline structure required fourteen principal actors to portray seven characters across two films, making it one of the most ambitious casting projects in recent horror cinema. Other notable Stephen King ensemble adaptations include The Stand (1994 miniseries and 2020 series) and The Shawshank Redemption (1994), but IT's combination of child and adult casts, along with its box-office success, has cemented its place in popular culture.

Why was Bill Skarsgård chosen as the Pennywise clown performer?

Director Andy Muschietti selected Bill Skarsgård for his ability to deliver an unsettling, otherworldly performance, including his natural ability to move his eyes independently, which added a uniquely terrifying quality to the character. Skarsgård auditioned with a self-taped performance that demonstrated his range of vocal tones and physical expressiveness. Muschietti has noted in interviews that Skarsgård's approach was less comedic and more alien than Tim Curry's 1990 portrayal, aligning with the darker tone of the 2017 and 2019 films. The actor also contributed to the character's design, collaborating with costume and makeup teams to refine Pennywise's appearance.

Are the child actors in IT Chapter Two?

Yes, all seven child actors return in IT Chapter Two for extensive flashback sequences that bridge the two timelines and provide crucial character development. These scenes were filmed during the production of Chapter Two, not during the original Chapter One shoot, allowing the young cast to age naturally and reflect the passage of time. The flashbacks serve both narrative and emotional functions, reinforcing the bond between the Losers Club members and deepening the audience's investment in their adult counterparts. The interplay between child and adult performances is a defining feature of the it film characters structure.
