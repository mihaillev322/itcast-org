About IT Cast: how we document the IT movie cast accurately

Our mission

IT Cast exists to provide a clear, reliable, and internationally accessible reference for the IT movie cast across both IT Chapter One (2017) and IT Chapter Two (2019). Whether you're researching Stephen King IT actors, verifying credits for the Losers Club ensemble, or exploring how the IT film characters map to their performers, this site offers factual, well-sourced information without hype or speculation.

We recognise that film credits can vary by region, release format, and database. Our goal is to cut through inconsistencies by cross-referencing reputable sources and presenting the IT remake actors list in a format that serves students, journalists, fans, and researchers worldwide. We focus on accuracy, clarity, and accessibility, ensuring that every visitor—regardless of their familiarity with horror film casting—can find the information they need quickly and confidently.

This site does not cover rumours, unverified casting news, or subjective rankings. Instead, we document the ensemble as it appears in the finished films, supported by authoritative references and a commitment to editorial integrity.

Method: how we verify credits

Every cast member listed on IT Cast is verified through a multi-step process. We begin by consulting official studio materials when available, including press kits, end credits, and promotional documentation. We then cross-check these details against reputable film databases, encyclopedic references, and established media outlets with editorial oversight.

When discrepancies arise—such as variations in character name spelling or actor credit order—we prioritise the most stable and widely cited sources. We do not rely on user-generated content alone, nor do we publish casting information based solely on social media announcements or unverified industry gossip.

Our editorial standard is simple:

Accuracy over speed. We would rather take the time to verify a credit properly than publish incomplete or misleading information.

This approach ensures that IT Cast remains a trustworthy resource for anyone seeking factual information about the IT cast, from academic researchers to casual viewers revisiting the films.

Contact and corrections

If you believe you have identified an error in our cast documentation, we welcome your feedback. Please email us at corrections@itcast.org with the following information:

  • The specific page and section where the error appears
  • A clear description of the issue
  • At least one authoritative source that supports your correction (such as a link to an official studio page, reputable database, or credible news outlet)

We review all correction requests carefully and will update the site promptly if we can verify the information through our editorial process. Please note that we cannot respond to requests that lack supporting evidence or that relate to subjective opinions rather than factual errors.

For general enquiries about the site, you may also use the email address above. We aim to respond within one week, though complex verification requests may take longer.
